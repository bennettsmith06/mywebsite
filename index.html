Senior Research Project

For my senior research project, I am working with Keepher to develop a longer-lasting latex coating for the palm of goalkeeper gloves. There is an inverse relationship between the lifetime and stickiness of latex. The stickier the latex coating, the quicker it wears away off the palm of the glove. My goal is to identify or synthesize a type of latex that maximizes stickiness without compromising lifetime.

The work I have done so far can be found here . I have determined that embossed latex coatings last longer than smooth latex coatings, but at the cost of being less sticky. Using infrared spectroscopy (FTIR), I have identified poly(butadiene:styrene) and styrene/isoprene as the types of latex Keepher uses to coat their embossed and smooth goalkeeper gloves, respectively. I have recommended using neoprene or polyurethane instead because these materials offer better abrasion resistance without compromising stickiness, according to information I have found in materials databases.

Composite Synthesis

I have experience synthesizing sheets of fiberglass composites. This picture shows the four sheets I used to create my composite material. The goal of this project was to create a fiberglass composite with maximum strength for use in wind turbine blades.

I used a combination of fiber types and orientations to acheive maximum strenth in all directions. From top to bottom, here are the fiber types and orientations: twill 45 degrees, unidirectional 90 degrees, twill 45 degrees, unidirectional 0 degrees. I used a 60/40 ratio by weight of fiber to resin to have the best balance of mechanical performance and aesthetic quality.
This project has ignited my fascination with composite materials. They have applications ranging from aerospace to sporting goods. I can see myself pursuing a career in composites manufacturing.

Data Visualization

Since May 2019, I have been a member of the Butala Research Group in the Materials Science and Engineering Department at the University of Florida. We focus on understanding unconventional battery materials. My role in the group is to process and plot x-ray diffraction (XRD) data that shows information about the structure of a material.

This plot uses 360 data sets to visualize changes in the long-range structure of a material. The processing and plotting of this data was accomplished in Jupyter Notebook with Python and its packages such as Pandas, Numpy, and Matplotlib.
I have developed many scripts to process and plot different types of XRD data. Some noteable projects include visualizing the structural changes in a novel battery material after many cycles, and the identification of structural features related to magnetic properties in an electronic material.
I have really enjoyed learning how to work with data in Python. I can see myself pursuing a career in data analytics.
